      <p>This highly adaptable, low spreading shrub features soft, fine textured, aromatic leaves with a scent reminiscent of bayberry candles. Smaller in stature than the larger wax myrtle, it also produces profuse dark silvery blue-gray berries on female plants. An excellent pruned hedge, screen or specimen. Provides good erosion control. Evergreen in mild climates. Aromatic Foliage. Easy Care. Dwarf Plant. North American Native Selection.</p>
