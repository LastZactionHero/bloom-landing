      <p>Fragrant creamy-white flat-top flowers followed by white fruit that darkens to pink before maturing to blue. Shiny deep-green foliage turns cinnabar-red in the fall. For best fruit display, plant in groups that allow cross-pollination. Attractive in shrub borders, hedges and foundation or street-side plantings. A good solution for low spots or at the damp edges of a stream, pond or water garden. Spring Flowering. Attracts Butterflies. Easy Care. Deer Resistant. Fall Color. Bird Friendly. Ornamental Berries.</p>
